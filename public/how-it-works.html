How it works

A simple making flow.

Every lamp starts with three interchangeable parts. Mix, match, and download — no guesswork, no wasted filament. Our modular system is built for repeatability, full component compatibility, and a smooth path from idea to finished print.

01

Choose a base.

Browse a growing library of base profiles — from minimal cylinders to weighted geometric forms. Every base is engineered to sit flush with any compatible shade, so the pairing you choose is guaranteed to work before you download a single file.

02

Pair a shade.

Select a shade that complements your base. Filter by silhouette, diffusion style, or material weight. The live 3D preview updates instantly so you can see the assembled lamp before committing — eliminating the trial-and-error that frustrates most custom lighting projects.

03

Select the right adapter.

Choose from LED, E27, or E14 fittings. Adapters snap into any shade without modification, so switching bulb types later is as simple as reprinting one small component. The same base and shade work across all three — that's the compatibility promise baked into every design.

04

Download and print.

Your STL files are ready the moment you confirm your configuration. Optimised for FDM printers with sensible default tolerances, the files arrive pre-sliced for standard nozzle sizes. Whether you're printing one lamp for your studio or a run of custom window treatments for a client, the process stays identical every time.

Modular by Design

Why modular design changes the workflow.

Most lamp design starts from zero — geometry, fittings, tolerances, all rebuilt each time. LampForge flips that model. By separating the base, shade, and adapter into interchangeable components, you work with a system rather than a blank canvas. Designers can focus on form rather than rebuilding common lamp logic from scratch, moving faster and with greater creative confidence.

Faster experimentation.

Swap a shade, change an adapter, preview the result instantly — all without starting over. Modular components let you iterate in minutes rather than hours, making exploration feel natural rather than costly. Your creative instinct stays in the lead.

Consistent visual language.

Every component is designed to speak the same formal vocabulary. Proportions, wall thicknesses, and connection geometry are pre-resolved — so mixing and matching across bases and shades always produces a coherent result, whether you're designing one custom window treatment or an entire product range.

Clear fitting support.

LED strips, E27 pendants, E14 candelabra — every adapter in the system is engineered to the correct electrical fitting standard. No guesswork, no custom tolerancing. You choose the fitting; the geometry handles the rest.

The result is a workflow where creative decisions stay creative. LampForge handles the structural logic — leaving you free to explore, refine, and ship.

Print-ready by design

Designed for real-world
print outcomes.

Every component in LampForge is built with fabrication in mind — from the first layer to the final fitting.

01

Surface character.

Layer lines aren't a flaw — they're the signature of the craft. LampForge models are designed so that the visible geometry of FDM printing becomes part of the lamp's visual identity. Ridged shades catch light in ways injection-moulded parts never could, giving each piece a warmth and texture that feels genuinely handmade.

02

Hardware fit.

Every shade and base in the LampForge library ships with adapter variants engineered for LED, E27, and E14 fittings. Tolerances are tuned for standard hardware you can source locally — no custom parts, no guesswork. Swap fittings between designs freely using the same interchangeable adapter system across the entire component catalogue.

03

Ready-to-make files.

Downloads arrive as clean, manifold-closed STL files — validated, oriented for optimal bed placement, and free of the common mesh errors that slow fabrication down. Whether you're running a home printer or a small production setup, you go from download to first layer without any file repair or prep work. Straightforward by design.
